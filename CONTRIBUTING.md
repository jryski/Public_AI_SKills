# Contributing

Thanks for your interest in improving Public AI Skills.

This repository is source-available for personal use, not open source. Contributions must preserve that licensing model.

## Contribution status

Contributions are welcome by issue or pull request, but acceptance is discretionary.

By submitting a contribution, you represent that:

- you created the contribution or have the rights needed to submit it;
- the contribution does not include confidential, proprietary, or restricted third-party material;
- the contribution does not knowingly include private data, secrets, credentials, or customer information;
- the contribution can be included in this repository under the project licensing model.

## Inbound rights

By submitting a contribution, you grant Jesse Ryski and successors a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free right to use, copy, modify, publish, distribute, sublicense, and commercially license the contribution as part of this project or related commercial offerings.

This inbound grant does not remove your own rights to use your original contribution elsewhere, but it allows the project owner to maintain the personal-use and paid organizational-use model without later rights conflicts.

## AI-generated contributions

AI-assisted contributions are allowed only when the submitter reviews, understands, and takes responsibility for the result.

Do not submit AI-generated material copied from a third-party source, a restricted prompt library, a confidential system, or a license-incompatible work.

## What makes a good skill contribution

A good skill should:

- solve a reusable class of problems;
- define when to invoke it;
- define when not to invoke it;
- convert philosophy into observable behavior;
- include safety, authority, or reversibility boundaries where relevant;
- be written clearly enough for a cold reader or AI system to apply;
- include examples when possible.
